2 LandRCBM_split3pools

This documentation is work in progress. Potential discrepancies and omissions may exist for the time being. If you find any, please contact us here.

2.1 Overview

LandRCBM_split3pools translates forest landscape dynamics simulated from LandR compatible with the spadesCBM. The module provides the annual increments for the three above ground biomass pools: merchantable, foliage, and other. It does so for the yield tables which are used during the spinup event of spadesCBM, and for the annual cohort data which are used for the annual event of spadesCBM.

2.2 Inputs

The inputs of LandRCBM_split3pools are a combination of outputs from LandR, data to convert total above ground biomass into biomass of merchantable, foliage and other wood pools, and outputs in spadesCBM.

The default inputs are for the Western Boreal Regional Integrated Assessment (RIA) study area. Most inputs are expected to be provided by other modules (CBMdefault, Biomass_core, Biomass_yieldTables, Biomass_borealDataPrep, etc.).

2.3 Module functioning

2.3.1 Events

LandRCBM_split3pools has 7 events, 3 of which are plotting events. Saved plots can be found in the ~/LandRCBM/outputs/[simulation name]/figures/LandRCBM_split3pools directory.

2.3.1.1 splitInit

This initial event splits the total above ground biomass of the yield tables and the initial landscape into biomass of each of the above ground carbon pools (merchantable, foliage, and other).

2.3.1.2 postSpinupAdjustBiomass

After the CBM spinup, the biomass in the aboveground (merchantable, foliage, and other) and belowground (coarse roots and fine roots) live carbon pools will not match the observed data. This event re-initializes these pools to match observed initial aboveground biomass.

2.3.1.3 annualIncrements

In this event, cohortData from the current simulation year is compared with the previous year and the difference in biomass for each cohort is calculated and converted into increments of the three aboveground carbon pools.

2.3.1.4 prepareCBMvars

Each year, the object cbm_vars is updated to incorporate the calculated annual aboveground increments, handle dead organic matter cohorts (cohort without aboveground biomass), new cohorts, and disturbed cohorts.

2.3.1.5 summarizeAGBPools

Here, the above ground biomass in each above ground carbon pool is summarized for each species across the landscape at each time step.

2.3.1.6 plotYC

This event plots the yield curves for a number of randomly selected pixel groups. The yield curves describe how aboveground carbon pools change with stand age.

2.3.1.7 plotMaps

This event maps the aboveground carbon stocks and increments at a regular interval.

2.3.1.8 plotSummaries

This simple plotting event plots the evolution of the aboveground biomass of each pool for each species and for the entire landscape at the end of the simulation.

2.3.2 Estimating merchantable proportion of stemwood

Merchantable wood is estimated in Boudewyn et al. (2007) with the following equation:

\(nonmerchfactor = k + a * b_m^b\) \(nonmerchfactor = b_{nm}/b_m\) \(b_{nm} = b_m + b_n\)

Where:

  • \(b_n\) is the stemwood biomass of live, nonmerchantable trees

  • \(b_m\) is the stemwood biomass of merchantable sized live trees

  • \(b_{nm}\) is the total stemwood biomass in live trees

  • \(k\), \(a\), and \(b\) are model parameters fit separately by jurisdiction, ecozone, and lead tree species (Table 4 in Boudewyn et al. (2007)).

Because it is not possible to analytically infer new parameters for the equation above, they had to be inferred through simulations resulting in a new exponential model with this form to estimate the mechantable proportion of stemwood:

\(b_m = k - e^{-a(b_{nm} - b)}\)

Where:

  • \(k\), \(a\), and \(b\) are model parameters inferred separately from those in Boudewyn et al. (2007)

If the value of \(b_m\) exceeds the simulated inferred \(cap\), then the value will default to the \(cap\) proportion like so:

\[\text{b_m} = \begin{cases} \text{b_m}, & \text{if b_m} > \text{cap} \\ \text{cap}, & \text{if b_m} \le \text{cap} \end{cases}\]

The merchantable proportion of stemwood biomass is calculated in this way for each species present in the simulation. Below is an example of some species along with a comparison to the estimates using the original Boudewyn et al. (2007) equation.
